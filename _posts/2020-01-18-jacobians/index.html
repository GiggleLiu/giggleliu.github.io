Jacobians and Hessians for Reversible Primitives

This blog covers the Jacobians and Hessians for reversible primitives, they can be used to propagate gradients and Hessians in a reversible programs.

The Definition

For function y⃗=f(x⃗)\vec{y} = f(\vec{x})y​=f(x), we define its Jacobian as

Jij=∂yi∂xj. J_{ij} = \frac{\partial y_i}{\partial x_j}. Jij​=∂xj​∂yi​​.

Its Hessian is

Hijk=∂ykxixj H^k_{ij} = \frac{\partial y_k}{x_i x_j} Hijk​=xi​xj​∂yk​​
